## What To Notice

Power in AI does not only live in the model itself. It also lives in supply chains, cloud services, proprietary hardware, standards, vendor contracts, maintenance systems, and repair pathways.

Infrastructure can shape what kinds of decisions are even possible.

## Questions To Ask

- Who owns or controls the critical infrastructure?
- What dependencies limit local oversight or repair?
- How do standards, vendors, or lock-in shape governance options?
- Who becomes more powerful as the system scales?

## Why This Matters

This pattern helps you treat infrastructure as political, not just technical background. It is especially useful in edge AI and neuromorphic contexts, where hardware choices and vendor ecosystems can directly shape governance and accountability.
