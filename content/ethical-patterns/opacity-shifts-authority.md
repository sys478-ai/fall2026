## What To Notice

Opacity is not just about a system being complicated. It is also about power. When a system becomes difficult to inspect or explain, some people gain more authority and other people lose the ability to question what is happening.

This is why opacity is not only a design problem. It is also a governance and accountability problem.

## Questions To Ask

- Who can meaningfully inspect or explain the system?
- Who is asked to trust the output without understanding it?
- What kinds of challenge or appeal are still possible?
- Does opacity protect legitimate complexity, or does it shield power?

## Why This Matters

This pattern gives you language for recognizing when complexity turns into deference. It helps you name when explanation is necessary for accountability, contestability, and public trust rather than treating opacity as something everyone simply has to accept.
