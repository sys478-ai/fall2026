## What To Notice

Classification systems do not simply discover natural groups that were already sitting there waiting to be found. They sort, label, and draw boundaries. Once those boundaries exist, they can shape who gets included, who gets excluded, and who gets treated as risky, normal, capable, suspicious, or in need of intervention.

When you see a system classifying something, do not just ask whether it is accurate. Ask what the categories are doing.

## Questions To Ask

- What categories does the system rely on?
- Who defined those categories, and for what purpose?
- What gets flattened or excluded when categories are imposed?
- Who is harmed when categories are treated as natural or obvious?

## Why This Matters

You will encounter this pattern early and often because anomaly detection, risk scoring, and many governance systems all depend on classifications. Those classifications can look technical and still carry deep social assumptions. Learning to notice that is one of the core habits of this course.
