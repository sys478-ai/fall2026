## What To Notice

A threshold is the point where a score becomes an action: a flag, a denial, a warning, an intervention, or a punishment. That line may look mathematical, but it has real consequences. Small differences in score can lead to very different treatment.

This is why thresholds are never just technical settings. They help distribute risk, error, and harm.

## Questions To Ask

- Where is the cutoff, and who chose it?
- What happens to people on either side of the line?
- Which errors are treated as tolerable, and for whom?
- Who is accountable when the threshold causes harm?

## Why This Matters

Use this pattern when you want to move from model output to lived consequence. It will help you ask better questions than "How accurate is the model?" Accuracy matters, but it does not tell you who absorbs the cost of error.
