## What To Notice

No anomaly exists without a baseline. Before a system can flag something as unusual, it has to define what counts as ordinary. That baseline is never automatic. It is built from data, assumptions, and judgments about acceptable behavior or expected conditions.

When you see the word "anomaly," ask yourself: anomalous compared to what?

## Questions To Ask

- How was normality defined in this system?
- Who or what supplied the baseline?
- What happens to people who differ from the baseline for legitimate reasons?
- When does anomaly detection become a tool of surveillance or social control?

## Why This Matters

This pattern sits at the center of the BRAID case study. It helps you see that anomaly detection is never only about technical deviation. It is also about who gets treated as outside the norm and what happens next.
