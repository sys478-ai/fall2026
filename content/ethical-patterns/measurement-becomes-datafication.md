## What To Notice

Measurement does not stop at sensing. Once something is measured, it can be stored, compared, tracked over time, linked to other records, and fed into new systems. That is part of what datafication means: turning more of life into something that can be organized and acted on as data.

What begins as measurement can become surveillance, evaluation, or automated governance.

## Questions To Ask

- What is being sensed, logged, or tracked?
- How does measurement change the thing being measured?
- What new forms of monitoring become possible once data exists?
- Who can access, reuse, or reinterpret these records later?

## Why This Matters

This pattern helps you connect sensors and instrumentation to broader questions of surveillance, governance, and institutional power. It keeps measurement from seeming like a neutral first step with no downstream effects.
