## What Happened

On November 30, 2022, OpenAI released ChatGPT as a publicly accessible web interface. The system was designed for conversational interaction: users could ask questions, request text generation, and have extended back-and-forth exchanges in natural language.

ChatGPT reached one million users within five days and 100 million users within two months — the fastest consumer technology adoption in history to that point. A wave of competing releases from Google, Meta, Anthropic, and others followed within months.

The release triggered rapid, overlapping debates. Educators raised concerns about academic integrity and the future of writing instruction. Journalists and researchers noted the system's tendency to produce confident but factually incorrect statements. Labor economists began debating which cognitive jobs were most exposed to automation. Companies integrated the underlying API into products.

The release also surfaced questions about how the system was built. A _Time_ magazine investigation published in January 2023 reported that OpenAI had contracted with Kenyan workers paying less than $2/hour to review and label harmful content — material involving violence, sexual abuse, and other disturbing content — used to train safety filters. These workers were not prominent in public accounts of the technology's development.

**Source:** Billy Perrigo, "OpenAI Used Kenyan Workers on Less Than $2 Per Hour to Make ChatGPT Less Toxic," _Time_, January 18, 2023.
