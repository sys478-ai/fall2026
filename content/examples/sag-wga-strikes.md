## What Happened

In May 2023, the Writers Guild of America went on strike against the Alliance of Motion Picture and Television Producers. In July 2023, SAG-AFTRA joined them. It was the first time both unions had struck simultaneously since 1960.

AI was among the central issues in both strikes. The WGA sought protections against studios using AI to generate or revise scripts, or using AI-generated material to undercut writers' pay. SAG-AFTRA sought prohibitions on studios digitally replicating actors' faces and voices without consent and compensation — including using brief scans of background actors to generate digital stand-ins for entire productions.

Studios initially offered to scan background actors once and use that likeness in perpetuity for the rate of a single day's work. This became one of the most widely discussed examples of what unlimited digital consent could mean in practice.

Both unions reached agreements in late 2023. The contracts included guardrails on AI use and requirements for consent and compensation for digital likenesses, though critics argued the protections were weaker than needed given how quickly the technology was developing.

The strikes are among the first major collective labor actions that explicitly named AI as a central issue — not a hypothetical future concern but an active dispute over existing and near-term capabilities.

**Source:** John Koblin, Mike Isaac, and Nicole Sperling, "Hollywood Strikes: What Writers and Actors Want," _The New York Times_, July 14, 2023.
