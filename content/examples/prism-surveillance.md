## What Happened

In June 2013, journalists working with documents provided by Edward Snowden published a series of stories revealing the existence of several large-scale NSA surveillance programs.

PRISM allowed the NSA to obtain data — email, documents, audio, video, photographs, connection logs — directly from the servers of major US technology companies including Microsoft, Yahoo, Google, Facebook, YouTube, Skype, AOL, and Apple. The program operated under Section 702 of the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act, which authorized surveillance of foreign nationals overseas but incidentally collected large amounts of US persons' communications.

Additional revelations disclosed bulk collection of telephone metadata from US telecommunications providers under Section 215 of the PATRIOT Act, affecting hundreds of millions of Americans.

The revelations triggered a global debate about surveillance, privacy, and the relationship between technology companies and government. Several companies published transparency reports for the first time; others invested in end-to-end encryption. The USA FREEDOM Act of 2015 constrained some of the bulk collection authorities revealed. Most of the fundamental authorities remain in place.

**Source:** Glenn Greenwald, "NSA Prism program taps in to user data of Apple, Google and others," _The Guardian_, June 6, 2013.
