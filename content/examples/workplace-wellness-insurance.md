## What Happened

Many large employers offer wellness programs that include biometric screenings (blood pressure, cholesterol, BMI), fitness tracking devices, mental health assessments, and health risk questionnaires. These programs are typically framed as benefits to employees — tools for supporting health and potentially earning premium discounts.

The data generated by these programs is not confined to the wellness vendor. Depending on program structure, it may flow to the employer's health insurance carrier, a benefits administrator, or other third-party data processors. In some cases, this information can inform how health risk is calculated for insurance pricing or benefit design.

Unlike health data collected by a physician under HIPAA protections, data collected through employer wellness programs may be governed by different rules depending on program design. Workers are often required to participate or face premium penalties, limiting the voluntariness of their consent.

**Source:** See ProPublica's reporting on employer wellness programs and data use. For the regulatory landscape, see the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission's wellness program rules and the ADA/GINA intersection.
