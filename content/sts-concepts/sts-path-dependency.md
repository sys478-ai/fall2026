## What To Notice

Governance should not wait until a system is already widespread and difficult to challenge. Anticipatory governance means asking hard questions early, before assumptions harden into infrastructure and before harms become normal.

This pattern encourages you to think ahead, not because the future is fully predictable, but because waiting can make democratic choice much narrower.

## Questions To Ask

- What assumptions are becoming embedded early?
- What harms are foreseeable even if evidence is incomplete?
- What dependencies or infrastructures could become hard to reverse later?
- What governance questions should be asked before scale arrives?

## Why This Matters

This pattern is central to the course because it connects technical novelty to democratic responsibility. It asks you to think not only about what a system can do, but also about what questions should be raised before it becomes ordinary.
