## What To Notice

AI systems are built from physical materials and supported by energy, water, fabrication, shipping, labor, maintenance, and disposal. The interface may look clean and almost immaterial, but the system never is.

This pattern asks you to look below the screen and behind the model.

## Questions To Ask

- What hardware and extraction make this system possible?
- What environmental claims are being made, and compared to what?
- Where do energy, water, and waste appear in the system lifecycle?
- What labor and repair infrastructures remain out of view?

## Why This Matters

This pattern is especially important for neuromorphic systems, where efficiency claims may be meaningful and important, but still need to be placed inside larger questions about materials, fabrication, lifecycle costs, and environmental tradeoffs.
