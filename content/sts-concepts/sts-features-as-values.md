## What To Notice

Systems do not take in everything. They work from selected inputs, features, and proxies. That means someone decided what would count as relevant evidence and what could be ignored.

If a system uses a proxy, slow down. A proxy is often a stand-in for something harder to measure directly, and stand-ins can carry hidden assumptions.

## Questions To Ask

- What inputs does the system treat as meaningful?
- What is being used as a proxy for something harder to measure?
- What important qualities are left out because they are inconvenient?
- How do feature choices advantage some interpretations over others?

## Why This Matters

You may first encounter machine learning as a technical pipeline with steps that seem clean and neutral. This pattern helps you see that feature selection is also interpretive. It reflects judgment, priorities, and institutional choices about what matters.
