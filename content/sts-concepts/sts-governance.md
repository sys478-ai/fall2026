## What To Notice

When people talk about governance, they often mean formal regulation by the state. But systems are also governed through procurement rules, standards, professional norms, audits, documentation, contracts, workplace policy, and institutional decisions about whether to adopt a tool at all.

This pattern helps you widen the frame so governance does not look like a single lever pulled too late.

## Questions To Ask

- What institutions shape this system besides government regulators?
- What standards, contracts, or procurement choices matter here?
- What documentation or audit practices exist?
- Who has the power to limit, redesign, or refuse the system before formal regulation appears?

## Why This Matters

If governance is imagined too narrowly, it can seem absent even when many meaningful choices are still open. This pattern helps you see the wider field of public and institutional action around AI systems.
