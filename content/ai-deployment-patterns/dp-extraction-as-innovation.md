## What To Notice

Innovation stories often highlight novelty, convenience, speed, personalization, or efficiency. Those benefits may be real, but they can also hide what is being taken, transferred, or made newly available for profit or control.

This pattern asks you to look for extraction underneath the language of progress.

## Questions To Ask

- What is being captured, scraped, measured, mined, or monetized?
- Whose data, labor, attention, creativity, environment, or public infrastructure makes the system valuable?
- Who receives the benefits, and who bears the costs?
- What forms of consent, compensation, refusal, repair, or public accountability are available?
- Does the system create value by making existing social resources easier to appropriate?

## Why This Matters

This pattern helps you pause before accepting innovation language at face value. It does not assume every new system is exploitative. Instead, it asks what kinds of value the system depends on, who controls that value, and whether people or communities have meaningful power over what is being taken from them.
