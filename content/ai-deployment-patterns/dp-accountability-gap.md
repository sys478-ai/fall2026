## What To Notice

Automation does not make responsibility disappear. Instead, it often spreads responsibility across people, organizations, devices, software, vendors, and maintenance systems. That can make it harder to tell who is actually answerable when something goes wrong.

If no one seems responsible, that is usually not because responsibility vanished. It is because it has been reorganized.

## Questions To Ask

- Who still makes meaningful decisions in this system?
- What responsibility shifts to vendors, operators, or maintainers?
- How are mistakes explained away as system behavior rather than human choice?
- What oversight exists when automated outputs shape action?

## Why This Matters

This pattern helps you move beyond the phrase "the system decided." It pushes you to ask what institutional arrangement allowed the automated output to matter and who still had the power to question, override, or redesign it.
