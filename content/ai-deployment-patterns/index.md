These cards describe recurring patterns in how AI systems operate in the world. Each one names something you can actually look for "in the wild" - a way that a system classifies, measures, infers, or acts on people that tends to appear across many different contexts and applications. The patterns here are not hypothetical risks or abstract concerns. They are things that have happened, are happening, and will happen again, because they follow from how AI systems are built and deployed. Use these cards when you are reading a case, analyzing a system, or trying to name what you are seeing. Many of these patterns have antecedents in earlier automated systems — part of the analysis is recognizing what is continuous with the past and what AI makes distinctively different.
