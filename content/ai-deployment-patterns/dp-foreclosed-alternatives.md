## What To Notice

When a system gets framed as the obvious, modern, or inevitable solution, other possibilities can begin to disappear from view. They may start to seem unrealistic, outdated, too slow, or not worth discussing.

This pattern helps you notice when the range of imaginable choices is shrinking.

## Questions To Ask

- What alternatives are being ignored or dismissed?
- Who benefits when one path is treated as inevitable?
- What forms of refusal, redesign, or non-adoption remain possible?
- How does the narrative of progress narrow democratic choice?

## Why This Matters

This pattern helps you recognize when a governance conversation has already been narrowed by assumptions about innovation, speed, or inevitability. Once you notice that narrowing, it becomes easier to ask what alternatives are being left out.
