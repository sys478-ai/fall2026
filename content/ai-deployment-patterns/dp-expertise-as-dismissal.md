## What To Notice

Technical knowledge matters, but it can also be used to shut down public questions. When a system is described as too complex for outsiders, policymakers, or affected communities to understand, expertise can become a tool for narrowing who gets a say.

This pattern asks you to notice when complexity is being used to inform public discussion and when it is being used to avoid it.

## Questions To Ask

- Who is being told they are not qualified to question the system?
- Is expertise being used to explain, or to exclude?
- What forms of participation or challenge are still possible for non-specialists?
- How can technical knowledge support democratic oversight rather than replace it?

## Why This Matters

Good governance requires expertise, but it also requires accountability. This pattern helps you recognize when technical authority is being used to limit democratic participation rather than to support better public judgment.
