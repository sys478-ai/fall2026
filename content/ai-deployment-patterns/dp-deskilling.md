## What To Notice

Automation can be useful, but it can also change what people know how to do for themselves. When systems take over parts of memory, judgment, navigation, or decision-making, people and institutions may become more dependent on those systems and less practiced at the underlying skill.

Support and dependency can grow at the same time.

## Questions To Ask

- What skills are being supported, replaced, or weakened?
- What happens when people rely on the system for routine judgment?
- Who can still operate effectively if the system fails?
- Does convenience today create dependency tomorrow?

## Why This Matters

This pattern helps you ask not only what AI makes easier, but also what it may weaken over time. It is a useful way to think about expertise, trust, institutional resilience, and what happens when systems fail.
