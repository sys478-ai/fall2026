## What To Notice

People often describe AI systems in human terms: they think, learn, understand, dream, become alive, or even deserve sympathy. Those descriptions can make systems feel more human-like than they are and can distract from questions about design, power, and accountability.

Metaphors matter because they shape what people think the system is.

## Questions To Ask

- What metaphors are shaping public interpretation here?
- What do those metaphors clarify, and what do they distort?
- Does talk of aliveness or sentience distract from material and institutional questions?
- How does anthropomorphism shift sympathy, trust, or responsibility?

## Why This Matters

This pattern is especially important in brain-inspired and neuromorphic contexts, where biological language can make technical systems seem more alive, more human-like, or more mysterious than they really are.
