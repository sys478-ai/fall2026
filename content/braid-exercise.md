This page gathers the parts of the SYS 478 site that are most useful for a focused BRAID-oriented exercise. It is designed as a short path through the course shell for building enough technical and socio-analytic grounding to engage neuromorphic anomaly detection as a case of anticipatory governance.

## How to Use This Playlist

Move through the materials in roughly this order:

1. **Conceptual recognition**: notice the kind of system you are dealing with.
2. **Technical grounding**: learn enough mechanism to ask sharper public questions.
3. **STS and recognition patterns**: connect technical features to power, institutions, and governance.
4. **Examples and analogies**: compare BRAID to more familiar AI systems.
5. **Anticipatory governance**: ask what should be examined before deployment norms are settled.

## 3. Suggested Exercise Flow

If you want to run this as a compact exercise or preparation sequence, use this path:

1. Review the course modules and topic pages above.
2. Work through the technical, STS, pattern, example, and governance cards below.
3. Note where the page marks a concept as **Missing**. Those are useful gaps for future development or discussion.
4. Compare BRAID to at least one familiar example from the example-card set.
5. Write a short response addressing:
   - What problem is this system trying to solve?
   - What counts as normal, anomalous, risky, or efficient?
   - What becomes harder to inspect, contest, or govern?
   - What oversight, documentation, or limits should be considered before broader deployment?
