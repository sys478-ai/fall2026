## Topic / Focus

This opening session surveys the landscape of AI mediation: the range of decisions, inferences, classifications, and nudges that AI systems are now performing across healthcare, education, employment, news, public safety, and personal life.

Rather than defining AI technically from the start, we ask where it shows up, what it does, and what stakes are involved. The goal is to build a shared vocabulary for the questions we will pursue all semester.

## Guiding Questions

- Where are AI systems making or shaping decisions in public and institutional life?
- What kinds of things is AI being asked to infer, classify, recommend, or automate?
- Who designs these systems, and who is affected by them?
- What questions should we carry with us into the rest of the course?

## In This Class

In this first meeting, we will:

- map the range of AI mediation across different sectors of public and institutional life
- identify common patterns in how AI systems are described, deployed, and justified
- introduce the five guiding questions that will structure the course
- establish that AI is not a single technology but a family of technical choices with public consequences

## Looking Ahead

The next class asks why AI has become a public and ethical issue — not just a technical one. The rest of Module 1 builds conceptual tools before we examine any technical mechanisms directly.
