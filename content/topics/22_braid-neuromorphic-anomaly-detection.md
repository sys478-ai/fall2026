## Topic / Focus

This topic looks at schools and other social institutions as places where AI quietly reshapes authority. In education, AI may appear in proctoring, tutoring, behavioral monitoring, automated feedback, administrative triage, and risk classification. Similar patterns also appear in welfare offices, housing systems, and other institutional settings that sort people and allocate attention.

These domains matter because they show how AI can intensify institutional definitions of normality, success, compliance, risk, and need. Systems introduced as efficient or supportive may also increase surveillance, narrow human judgment, or make already unequal institutions feel more objective than they really are.

By the end of this topic, you should be able to explain how AI changes institutional judgment and why schools and similar settings are especially important for thinking about fairness, development, and power.

## Guiding Questions

- How is AI used in schools and similar institutions?
- What kinds of behavior or performance become newly legible to institutions?
- When do support systems become monitoring systems?
- How do institutional AI tools shape definitions of success, compliance, or risk?
- Why are schools a particularly revealing site for studying AI power?
