## Topic / Focus

This topic looks at AI in the worlds of news, politics, and public attention. Recommendation systems, generative media, search ranking, moderation, and political messaging all help shape what people see, what they trust, and what becomes visible or believable in public life.

These systems matter because they do not only distribute information. They also distribute attention, legitimacy, and narrative power. A political AI case is rarely just about content quality. It is also about amplification, control over visibility, and the tension between public discourse and platform incentives.

By the end of this topic, you should be able to explain how AI systems participate in shaping public attention and why that matters for politics, journalism, and democratic life.

## Guiding Questions

- How do AI systems shape what people see and believe?
- Who controls visibility, amplification, and narrative reach?
- What role do recommendation and generative systems play in politics?
- How does AI complicate trust in news and public information?
- What happens when platform incentives shape public discourse?

## In This Class

In this meeting, we will:

- connect AI media systems to public legitimacy and democratic life
