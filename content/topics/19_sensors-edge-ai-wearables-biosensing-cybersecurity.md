## Topic / Focus

This topic opens Module 4 by looking at work and employment as one of the most visible domains where AI systems reshape everyday life. AI now appears in hiring, resume filtering, scheduling, productivity tracking, performance scoring, workplace surveillance, and forms of algorithmic management that change how labor is organized and evaluated.

This case matters because it shows several course themes at once: categories and baselines, prediction, thresholds, error, and centralization of power. It also makes clear that AI is not only about replacing work. Often it is about intensifying supervision, redistributing discretion, and changing who gets to define what counts as good performance.

By the end of this topic, you should be able to explain how AI changes work not only through automation but also through monitoring, evaluation, and management.

## Guiding Questions

- How is AI used in hiring and workplace management?
- What counts as productivity, fit, or good performance in these systems?
- Who gains control when work becomes more measurable?
- How do these systems affect worker autonomy, dignity, or opportunity?
- When does efficiency become surveillance?

## In This Class

In this meeting, we will:

- examine employment and workplace AI case studies
- discuss hiring, scoring, monitoring, and algorithmic management
- identify who benefits from workplace automation and who bears new constraints
- connect labor cases to course themes of thresholds, error, and accountability

## Recognition Patterns

- AI can create dependency, cognitive offloading, and deskilling.
- Automation changes accountability.
- Power can concentrate in infrastructure.
