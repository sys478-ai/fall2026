## Topic / Focus

This topic closes Module 4 by returning to technological solutionism in a more grounded way. After looking at work, politics, healthcare, schools, and infrastructure, students ask when AI is being treated as the answer to problems that are actually institutional, political, or collective in nature.

The question is not whether AI can ever help. It is whether technical intervention is being used to substitute for other forms of action: labor reform, democratic regulation, stronger institutions, redistribution, care work, or public accountability. This topic helps students step back from individual systems and examine the broader narrative that smarter tools will fix social life.

By the end of this topic, you should be able to explain what technological solutionism is, why it is attractive, and why it can narrow the range of alternatives people are willing to imagine.

## Guiding Questions

- When is AI framed as the answer to a problem that is not mainly technical?
- What alternatives disappear when solutionist narratives take over?
- Why are claims of efficiency, safety, or optimization so persuasive?
- What kinds of institutional or political work can AI displace?
- How should we evaluate AI systems without assuming that more technology is always better?

## In This Class

In this meeting, we will:

- compare solutionist narratives across several domains
- identify what kinds of nontechnical alternatives are being sidelined
- discuss how AI narrows or expands institutional imagination
- use Module 4 cases to prepare for the governance work in Module 5

## Recognition Patterns

- Emerging technologies require anticipatory governance.
- Alternatives can be foreclosed.
- Progress narratives can hide extraction and control.

## BRAID / ELSI Connection

BRAID can be framed as efficient, innovative, and useful for early detection, but this topic asks a harder question: what kinds of problems are being defined as best solved by anomaly-detection hardware, and what other institutional or collective responses might still matter?
