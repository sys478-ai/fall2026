## Topic / Focus

This session brings the BRAID throughline to the foreground. Throughout the semester, students have been building toward a question: how do you govern an AI system whose deployment contexts, use cases, and risks are still being defined?

BRAID (Brain-Inspired AI for Distributed systems) is a neuromorphic anomaly detection system. We have examined its technical mechanisms, its borrowing of biological metaphors, its construction of normality, and its potential deployment in healthcare, schools, and public safety. Now we ask: what would it mean to govern this system responsibly before its harms become locked in?

This is a practice session for the final presentations: students apply the full analytical toolkit to a real case.

## Guiding Questions

- What do we now know about how BRAID works, and what remains uncertain?
- Who are the stakeholders? Who benefits, and who bears the risks?
- What governance tools are available, and which seem most appropriate?
- What would responsible deployment look like in one specific institutional context?
- What questions should regulators, designers, and affected communities be asking?

## In This Class

In this meeting, we will:

- synthesize what we have learned about BRAID as a socio-technical system
- map stakeholders, potential harms, and governance questions across institutional contexts
- apply NIST, EU AI Act, and other frameworks to neuromorphic anomaly detection
- draft and discuss governance recommendations as practice for the final presentations
