## Topic / Focus

This topic focuses on centralization and control. AI systems do not operate only through models. They also depend on infrastructures: cloud providers, chip makers, platform operators, standards bodies, software ecosystems, and repair pathways. Those infrastructures shape who has power to build, deploy, patch, audit, or contest a system.

This matters because public debate often focuses on model behavior while overlooking the political economy underneath. Control over hardware, vendors, standards, and maintenance can centralize decision-making power even when a system looks distributed or neutral at the interface.

By the end of this topic, you should be able to explain how AI infrastructures concentrate power and why governance has to include vendors, standards, dependency, and contestability.

## Guiding Questions

- Who controls the infrastructure on which AI depends?
- How do vendors and platforms shape what institutions can or cannot do?
- What happens when repair, patching, or auditing are limited?
- Why does dependency on infrastructure matter for public accountability?
- How should power and control enter AI governance conversations?
