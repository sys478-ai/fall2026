## Topic / Focus

This topic opens Module 3 by introducing the neuroscience vocabulary most relevant to later brain-inspired computing discussions: neurons, synapses, plasticity, signaling, timing, and adaptation. The goal is not to turn students into neuroscientists. It is to give them enough grounding to evaluate later analogies responsibly.

Neuroscience can illuminate technical design, but it can also be oversold. Brain language often carries public authority, which makes it important to ask what a biological analogy clarifies and what it distorts.

By the end of this topic, you should be able to explain a few core neuroscience ideas and describe why biological inspiration does not automatically justify broad claims about intelligence or realism.

## Guiding Questions

- What are neurons and synapses doing, in broad terms?
- What does plasticity mean?
- Why are timing and signaling so important in biological systems?
- What can technical designers learn from neuroscience?
- What gets distorted when brain metaphors travel too easily into AI discourse?

## Recognition Patterns

- AI systems can encourage anthropomorphism, aliveness, or sentience claims.

## BRAID / ELSI Connection

BRAID uses brain-inspired ideas, so students need a way to talk about those inspirations carefully. This topic establishes that neuroscience can be a source of useful design ideas without becoming a shortcut for hype or misplaced authority.
