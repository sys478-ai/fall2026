## Topic / Focus

This topic opens Module 2 by asking how messy parts of the world become usable data for machine learning systems. Before a model can classify, predict, or detect anything, someone has to decide what will be observed, how it will be sensed or recorded, how it will be measured, what counts as relevant signal, and which categories will organize the result.

That translation is never automatic. Sensors, forms, interfaces, logs, and measurement devices all help determine what becomes data in the first place. Concepts such as risk, attention, anomaly, productivity, or trustworthiness do not arrive as ready-made technical objects. They have to be turned into variables, labels, features, and baselines. In the process, some dimensions of reality become more visible while others get flattened or excluded.

By the end of this topic, you should be able to explain how measurement and categorization turn parts of the world into data and why that process is already value-laden before any model is trained.

## Guiding Questions

- What role do categories, labels, and baselines play in data creation?
- What gets made visible through measurement?
- What gets left out or flattened when the world becomes data?
- Why are proxies and features never neutral?
