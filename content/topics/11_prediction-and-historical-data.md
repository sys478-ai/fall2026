## Topic / Focus

This topic asks what happens after a model produces an output. Scores do not remain abstract for long. They become thresholds, rankings, flags, categories, and recommendations that shape what institutions or users do next.

This is where uncertainty becomes consequential. A probability or score may look technical, but once it is tied to action, it redistributes attention, opportunity, risk, and responsibility. A low score, high score, or anomaly flag is meaningful only because someone has decided what should happen next.

By the end of this topic, you should be able to explain how model outputs become decisions and why thresholds are governance choices as well as technical ones.

## Guiding Questions

- What is the difference between a score and a decision?
- How do thresholds turn uncertainty into action?
- Who decides where a threshold should be set?
- What kinds of decisions feel low stakes but are not?
- Why do rankings and risk scores deserve scrutiny?

## BRAID / ELSI Connection

BRAID and other anomaly-detection systems depend on thresholds that decide when a signal becomes important enough to flag, escalate, log, or ignore. Those choices shape both technical performance and downstream accountability.
