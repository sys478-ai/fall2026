## Topic / Focus

This topic asks why neural networks often inspire both excitement and unease. Their performance can be powerful, but their internal operations are often difficult to interpret in plain language. That opacity can shift authority toward designers, institutions, or vendors and away from the people affected by the system.

Trust is not only a matter of accuracy. It also involves contestability, explanation, and whether people can understand enough about a system to challenge it, refuse it, or use it responsibly. Neural networks make those questions more visible because their complexity can encourage both over-trust and resignation.

By the end of this topic, you should be able to explain why neural networks can be difficult to inspect or contest and why that matters for public use.

## Guiding Questions

- What kinds of opacity do neural networks create?
- Why can strong performance still leave people with weak understanding?
- When does opacity shift authority away from affected people?
- How can dependence on hard-to-interpret systems change judgment and responsibility?
- What kinds of trust should a system earn?

## Recognition Patterns

- Opacity shifts authority.
- AI can create dependency, cognitive offloading, and deskilling.
