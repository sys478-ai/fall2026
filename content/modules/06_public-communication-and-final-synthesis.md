This module is about synthesis and communication. Students refine public-facing explanations that connect technical mechanisms to stakeholder concerns, risks, and governance questions.

The work is not only presentation practice. Students revisit the course's central pattern: technical choices become social consequences when they are embedded in institutions, infrastructures, and public decisions.

For BRAID, the module asks students to explain neuromorphic anomaly detection clearly enough that non-specialists can understand both the technical promise and the governance stakes. The goal is communication that is accurate, accessible, and useful for decision-making.
