This module asks what happens when AI systems move from technical possibility into major domains of social life. Rather than organizing the material mainly by mechanism, the module turns to case studies: work and employment, news and politics, healthcare and biosensing, schools and other institutions, and the redistribution of power and control.

The goal is to help you see that AI does not arrive in the world as a neutral tool. It enters already-structured institutions with uneven power, competing incentives, and histories of inequality. In each domain, you will ask not only what the system does, but who benefits, who is monitored, who loses discretion, who gains control, and what kinds of political or institutional questions are being reframed as technical ones.

By the end of this module, you should be better able to connect technical systems to real institutional settings and to ask how detection, efficiency, automation, and adaptation can reshape labor, oversight, authority, and public ideas about what kinds of problems technology should solve.
