## What To Notice

Predictions are often described as if they are about the future alone. In practice, they rely heavily on the past: old records, inherited categories, prior judgments, and institutional habits that have already shaped what the data looks like.

That means a predictive system can carry earlier inequalities forward, even when it claims to be forward-looking.

## Questions To Ask

- What past data is being carried forward here?
- What historical inequities are embedded in the records?
- How might the system intensify the patterns it claims merely to observe?
- What happens when people are judged by past conditions they did not choose?

## Why This Matters

This pattern is especially important for understanding feedback loops and predictive governance. It helps you see how data-driven systems can reproduce older harms in newer, more technical-looking forms.
