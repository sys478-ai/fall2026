## Overview

This module focuses on organizational culture, unwritten rules, professional expectations, and boundaries. You will think about how to learn the culture of a workplace or graduate program while also deciding what professional authenticity means for you.

Every organization has a culture. Some expectations are clearly stated, but others are learned by observing, asking questions, and paying attention. The goal is not to judge a culture as good or bad. The goal is to notice whether it fits your needs, values, and preferences.

## In-Class Activities

### 1. Unwritten Rules Of An Organization

Use the [Uncovering the Unwritten Rules of an Organization worksheet](/fall2026/documents/module07/Uncovering%20the%20Unwritten%20Rules%20of%20an%20Organization.pdf) to think about organizational culture at UNC Asheville.

Consider:

<!-- .compact -->

- What did you know about UNC Asheville before you arrived?
- What did you only learn after becoming a student?
- What expectations are spoken, hidden, or learned through experience?
- What subcultures exist within the larger university?

Then choose another organization you have been part of in the last two years, such as a job, internship, club, volunteer group, church, team, or campus organization. With a partner, discuss the written and unwritten expectations in that setting.

### 2. Know Before You Go

Before interviewing or joining an organization, look for clues about its culture. Possible sources include:

<!-- .compact -->

- networking conversations
- informational interviews
- job descriptions
- organization websites
- social media
- employee reviews
- former employees or interns

Brainstorm what you would want to know before deciding whether an organization is a good fit for you.

### 3. Common Workplace Expectations

Discuss common expectations that show up in many organizations, such as:

<!-- .compact -->

- arriving on time
- communicating schedule changes early
- listening before speaking
- using professional written communication
- dressing appropriately for the setting
- learning how decisions are made

The details will vary by organization. The point is to notice expectations early so you can decide how to navigate them.

### 4. Boundaries and Professional Authenticity

Use the [Setting Boundaries handout](/fall2026/documents/module07/Setting%20Your%20Boundaries%20and%20Communicating%20Your%20Needs%20at%20Work.pdf) to reflect on how you want to show up at work. Consider:

<!-- .compact -->

- What parts of yourself do you want to bring into professional settings?
- What boundaries matter to you?
- What needs might you have to communicate at work?
- How might your answers change depending on the organization?

Prepare a short summary presentation for your peers. Use the format or artifact of your choice, such as an e-portfolio, website, LinkedIn profile, slides, podcast, poster, or another format.

Answer at least four of the following questions:

1. What are your possible lives? Which one feels most likely right now?
1. What were three takeaways from the budgeting and career research module?
1. How do you answer "Tell me about yourself"?
1. What was your initial SMART goal, and what progress did you make?
1. What LinkedIn profile inspired you, and how did it shape your own profile?
1. What are three strengths, skills, experiences, or accomplishments you have developed at UNC Asheville?
1. What did you learn from a networking or informational interview conversation?
