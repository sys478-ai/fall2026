## Overview

This final career module brings together the work you have done across the semester: values, possible lives, budgeting, salary research, durable skills, career stories, LinkedIn, networking, informational interviews, workplace culture, and goal setting.

Today, you will share a short synthesis of your career-readiness work and set a new goal for the next one to six months.

## Before Class

Complete the peer summary presentation assigned in [Career Module 7](/fall2026/assignments/career-module07). We will use that presentation in class today.

## In-Class Activities

### 1. Peer Presentations

Share your synthesis presentation with the class. As others present, listen for useful ideas, common themes, and resources you may want to use.

Use the <a href="/fall2026/documents/module08/Final%20Presentation%20Listening%20Guide.docx" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Final Presentation Listening Guide</a> as you listen. Ask questions that help your peers clarify next steps, name strengths, or identify resources.

### 2. Next Steps and Goal Setting

Using <a href="/fall2026/documents/module08/Next%20Steps%20%26%20Goal%20Setting.docx" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">this goal setting worksheet</a>, please complete the following:

> ### Reflect On Your SMART Goal
>
> In small groups, return to the SMART goal you set in Career Module 2.
>
> <!-- .compact -->
>
> - What worked well?
> - What challenges did you run into?
> - What resources did you use?
> - What resources could have helped?
> - Was this the right goal for you?

### 3. Set A New Goal

Set a new goal for the next one to six months.

As you write the goal, identify resources that could help you follow through. These might include personal systems, friends, family, faculty, the Career Center, alumni, mentors, or community contacts.

## What To Submit

After class, submit your final career-readiness synthesis artifact and a short reflection, about 300-500 words, that includes:

1. Which four presentation questions you answered.
1. One thing you learned from preparing or presenting your synthesis.
1. One thing you learned from a peer.
1. A new goal for the next one to six months.
1. At least one resource that can help you pursue that goal.
