## Overview

This module helps you turn your strengths, skills, experiences, and accomplishments into short career stories. These stories can be used in interviews, networking conversations, cover letters, graduate school applications, LinkedIn profiles, and promotion conversations.

## In-Class Activities

### 1. Tell Your Career Stories

Use the experiences and durable skills you identified in the previous career module. Draft five short stories that show your skills in action, using [this worksheet](/fall2026/documents/module05/Tell%20Your%20Career%20Stories.docx).

Each story should include:

1. **Background:** What was the situation or task?
1. **Action:** What did you do?
1. **Result:** What happened, what did you accomplish, or what did you learn?

After drafting, pair up and practice telling your stories. As you listen to your partner, notice what is clear, what needs more detail, and how the story could be tailored for a job interview, graduate school interview, or networking conversation.

### 2. Practice "Tell Me About Yourself"

Draft a short answer to the interview prompt "Tell me about yourself." Think of it as answering: "Tell me about yourself in relation to why you are a good fit for this opportunity."

A useful structure is:

1. Start with your present moment.
1. Highlight a few relevant experiences, skills, or accomplishments.
1. Connect those experiences to what you want to do next.

Practice your answer with a partner. It may feel awkward at first, but practicing aloud makes it easier to answer with confidence.

### 3. SMART Goal Check-In

Return to the SMART goal you set earlier in the semester.

<!-- .compact -->

- What progress have you made?
- If you have not made progress, what changes would help?
- Does the entire goal need to change, or only the steps toward it?
- If you have completed the goal, do you want to set another goal?

After you complete the homework, submit your Big Interview practice assignment and a short reflection, about 300-500 words, that includes:

1. One career story you drafted.
1. One thing you learned from practicing "Tell me about yourself."
1. One part of your interview answer you want to keep improving.
1. A brief update on your SMART goal.
