## Overview

This module asks you to imagine several possible futures instead of trying to choose one final answer. You will look for patterns across those possibilities and turn one next step into a SMART goal.

## In-Class Activities

### 1. Three Possible Lives

Open the [Three Possible Lives worksheet](/fall2026/documents/module02/Three%20Possible%20Lives.docx). Sketch three possible paths:

1. A likely path.
1. A path you are curious about.
1. A wildcard path with fewer limits.

For each path, note what it might look like, what values or interests show up, and what questions it raises.

### 2. Small Group Discussion

In small groups, share what you are comfortable sharing. As you listen and talk, consider:

- What themes show up across your three possibilities?
- What would you like to explore next?
- What resources, conversations, or experiences could help you learn more?

### 3. Final Reflection

Complete the final reflection on the worksheet.

### 4. SMART Goal

Open the [SMART Goal worksheet](/fall2026/documents/module02/SMART%20Goal.docx).

Set one career or post-graduation goal for this semester. Your goal should be SMART:

- **Specific:** What exactly do you want to do?
- **Measurable:** How will you know you completed it?
- **Attractive:** Why does it matter to you?
- **Realistic:** What makes this goal possible this semester?
- **Timebound:** What is your deadline?

## What To Submit

After class, submit your completed reflection, including:

1. A brief summary of your three possible lives.
1. One or two themes you noticed across those possible futures.
1. One question or resource you want to explore next.
1. One SMART goal for this semester.
