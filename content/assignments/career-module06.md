## Overview

This module focuses on professional connections. You will create or update a LinkedIn profile, practice reaching out to people, and plan an informational interview with someone connected to a field, organization, or path you want to learn more about.

## In-Class Activities

### 1. Create Or Update LinkedIn

Log in to LinkedIn or create an account. Browse a few profiles connected to careers you admire and notice what works well. Also consult the <a href="https://drive.google.com/file/d/1nJvvwgCK5JI7IHsDIKnjxRwcnApLBfa2/view?usp=drive_link" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">LinkedIn Guide</a>

Then create or update your own profile. As you work, consider:

- Who do you hope will read your profile?
- How can you use language from your "Tell me about yourself" practice?
- Which durable skills, experiences, or accomplishments should appear?
- Do you need a headshot or updated profile photo?
  - You can visit the Career Center to use the self-service headshot kiosk with your own cell phone, or you can <a href="https://unca.joinhandshake.com" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">schedule a professional headshot in Handshake</a>.

When your profile is ready for feedback, share it with a partner.

### 2. Make Initial Connections

Please consult the [Making Human Connections worksheet](/fall2026/documents/module06/Making%20Connections.docx) to think about how networking might help you. Start with people you already know: friends, coworkers, supervisors, faculty, classmates, mentors, or alumni.

Add a few connections on LinkedIn and draft a short message to someone you might want to learn from.

### 3. Plan an Informational Interview

Using [this worksheet](/fall2026/documents/module06/Updated%20-%20Informational%20Interview%20Guide.pdf) as a reference, choose at least two new people you could contact for informational interviews. These should be people who can help you learn more about an organization, role, field, graduate path, location, or professional community.

Draft a short outreach message. Ask for a brief conversation, explain that you are a student seeking advice, and name what you hope to learn.

Share your message with a partner and revise it based on feedback.

## Homework: Informational Interview

For homework, you must reach out to at least two **new connections** and try to schedule an informational interview.

- When you reach out to someone, ask for a little bit of their time to learn more about their organization, what they do, and how to get started in the field. Most professionals enjoy meeting with students because they remember what it was like when they were first starting out. Be sure to tell them that you are a student asking for advice.
- You will write a reflection based on the conversation you have with your new connection (details below).
- After the conversation, send a brief thank-you note. If appropriate, ask who else they recommend you talk with.

You must complete your informational interview within one month. Scheduling can take time, so start reaching out now rather than waiting until the deadline is close.

## What To Submit

After completing your homework informational interview, submit a reflection of at least 500 words. Include:

1. Who you reached out to and why.
1. How you found the person.
1. What you learned about their career journey.
1. How the conversation connects to your current post-graduation plans.
1. One next connection or next step you want to pursue.
