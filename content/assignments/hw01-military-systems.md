## Overview

This page contains four possible military-system scenarios involving a neuromorphic edge chip designed for anomaly detection.

Your group should choose **one** scenario for your deeper analysis.

For your chosen scenario, pay attention to:

- what the chip is monitoring
- whether the model appears to be fixed, adaptive, or hybrid
- what happens after an anomaly is flagged
- who is affected by the system's output

For any scenario, the model might be:

- **Fixed:** calibrated before deployment and not substantially changed unless manually updated.
- **Adaptive:** updated over time based on what the device encounters in use.
- **Hybrid:** allowed to adapt within preset limits or only after review.

Do not try to analyze the entire military domain. Choose one specific scenario.

## Scenario 1: Soldier Physiological Monitoring

A soldier wears a device that contains a neuromorphic edge chip. The device monitors physiological signals such as body temperature, heart rate, movement patterns, hydration-related signals, or other indicators of physical stress.

The device is intended to operate during training, transport, patrol, or field operations, including periods with limited connectivity.

When the system detects a pattern that differs from what it expects, it may alert the soldier, a medic, a squad leader, or a remote command system. It may also log the event for later review.

## Scenario 2: Humanitarian Aid Site Monitoring

A military unit or peacekeeping force helps manage security and logistics at a refugee camp or disaster-relief site. Temporary sensors containing neuromorphic edge chips are placed near supply depots, food distribution lines, medical tents, or water access points.

The sensors monitor patterns such as movement, crowd density, sound, heat, vibration, or equipment activity. The system is intended to operate in a setting with limited power, unreliable connectivity, and rapidly changing conditions.

When the system detects a pattern that differs from what it expects, it may alert aid workers, military personnel, camp staff, or emergency responders. It may also log the event, activate additional sensing, or mark an area for human review.

## Scenario 3: Autonomous or Semi-Autonomous Drone Monitoring

A drone or ground vehicle contains a neuromorphic edge chip that monitors movement, sensor patterns, environmental conditions, or system behavior while operating in a conflict zone.

The system may operate with intermittent communication to a human operator. It may be used for reconnaissance, navigation, search, threat detection, or support for other systems.

When the system detects a pattern that differs from what it expects, it may alert an operator, change route, mark an area for review, request confirmation, or trigger another system response depending on how it is designed.
