## Overview

This module helps you name your strengths, skills, and accomplishments in language you can use for resumes, interviews, networking, graduate school applications, and salary conversations.

Employers have increasingly emphasized <a href="https://www.naceweb.org/job-market/trends-and-predictions/employer-use-of-skills-based-hiring-practices-grows" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">skills-based hiring</a>. Today, you will connect your own experiences to durable skills and compare those skills with the language used in job postings.

## In-Class Activities

### 1. Durable Skills Introduction

Review the [durable skills handout](/fall2026/documents/module04/Durable%20Skills%20.docx). Mark skills you recognize in yourself and note one experience that shows each skill.

### 2. Favorite Experiences At UNC Asheville

Use the [Favorite Experiences worksheet](/fall2026/documents/module04/Favorite%20Experiences.docx). Pair up, interview each other, and listen for durable skills connected to the experiences described.

By the end, identify your top five durable skills.

### 3. Relating Skills To The Future

Use [The Durable Skills I Need worksheet](/fall2026/documents/module04/The%20Durable%20Skills%20I%20Need.docx).

Open [Handshake](https://unca.joinhandshake.com/) and review at least three job postings or job titles that interest you. Look for durable skills, technical skills, and repeated words across postings.

Identify where your current skills align and where you might want to grow.

### 4. SMART Goal Check-In

Return to the SMART goal you set earlier in the semester.

<!-- .compact -->

- What progress have you made?
- If you have not made progress, what changes would help?
- Does the entire goal need to change, or only the steps toward it?
- If you have completed the goal, do you want to set another goal?

## What To Submit

After class, submit a short reflection, about 300-500 words, that includes:

1. Your top five durable skills from the favorite experiences activity.
1. One experience that demonstrates at least two of those skills.
1. Three job postings or job titles you reviewed.
1. Skills you already have that match those postings.
1. One skill you want to build.
1. A brief update on your SMART goal.
