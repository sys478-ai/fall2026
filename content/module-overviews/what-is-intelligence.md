---
title: 'What Is Intelligence?'
excerpt: 'This module opens the course by treating intelligence as a contested social, technical, and political category.'
---

This module asks what counts as intelligence, who gets to define it, and what happens when definitions become embedded in tests, datasets, institutions, and technical systems.

Students begin with familiar claims about human, animal, machine, social, and embodied intelligence. From there, the module shifts toward measurement: how categories are made, how they travel through data, and how they can appear neutral after their social choices have been hidden.

The module also introduces the course's BRAID throughline. Terms such as signal, noise, normal, anomaly, and adaptation are not only technical labels. They are categories that shape what a system can detect, what it ignores, and who becomes responsible for the consequences.
