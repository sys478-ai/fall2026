This module is the culminating presentation sequence for the course. Students bring together technical explanation, ethical analysis, stakeholder reasoning, and governance recommendations.

The final work should show how a concrete AI system operates and why that operation matters socially. Students are expected to make mechanisms understandable without flattening their complexity, and to connect risks or benefits to practical questions of oversight, accountability, and public value.

For BRAID-related projects, this means explaining neuromorphic anomaly detection as both a hardware-level technical approach and a public issue involving energy, surveillance, auditability, misuse, and anticipatory governance.
